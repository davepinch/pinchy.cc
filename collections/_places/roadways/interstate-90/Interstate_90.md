**Interstate 90** (**I-90**) is an eastâ€“west transcontinental freeway and the longest Interstate Highway in the [United States](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States) at 3,021 miles (4,862 km). It begins in [Seattle](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle), Washington, and travels through the Pacific Northwest, Mountain West, Great Plains, Midwest, and the Northeast, ending in [Boston](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boston), Massachusetts. The highway serves 13 states and has 16 auxiliary routes, primarily in major cities such as [Chicago](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago), Cleveland, Buffalo, and Rochester.
