**Altair** is the brightest star in the constellation of [Aquila](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aquila_(constellation)) and the twelfth-brightest star in the night sky. It has the Bayer designation Alpha Aquilae, which is Latinised from **α Aquilae** and abbreviated **Alpha Aql** or **α Aql**. Altair is an A-type main-sequence star with an apparent visual magnitude of 0.77 and is one of the vertices of the [Summer Triangle](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Summer_Triangle) asterism; the other two vertices are marked by [Deneb](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deneb) and [Vega](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vega). It is located at a distance of 16.7 light-years (5.1 parsecs) from the Sun.  Altair is currently in the G-cloud—a nearby interstellar cloud, an accumulation of gas and dust.
