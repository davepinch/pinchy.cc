**Mississippi** (/ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpi/) is a state in the Southeastern region of the United States, bordered to the north by [Tennessee]({% include cc-url for="Tennessee - Wikipedia" %}); to the east by [Alabama]({% include cc-url for="Alabama - Wikipedia" %}); to the south by the [Gulf of Mexico]({% include cc-url for="Gulf of Mexico - Wikipedia" %}); to the southwest by [Louisiana]({% include cc-url for="Louisiana - Wikipedia" %}); and to the northwest by [Arkansas]({% include cc-url for="Arkansas - Wikipedia" %}). Mississippi's western boundary is largely defined by the [Mississippi River]({% include cc-url for="Mississippi River - Wikipedia" %}). Mississippi is the 32nd largest by area and 35th-most populous of the 50 U.S. states and has the lowest per-capita income in the United States. Jackson is both the state's capital and largest city. Greater Jackson is the state's most populous metropolitan area, with a population of 591,978 in 2020.
