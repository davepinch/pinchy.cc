**San Juan County** (Spanish: Condado de San Juan) is a county located in the Salish Sea in the far northwestern corner of the U.S. state of [Washington]({% include cc-url for="Washington (state) - Wikipedia" %}). As of the 2020 census, its population was 17,788. The county seat and only incorporated city is Friday Harbor, located on San Juan Island. The county was formed on October 31, 1873, from [Whatcom County]({% include cc-url for="Whatcom County, Washington - Wikipedia" %}) and is named for the San Juan Islands, which are in turn named for Juan Vicente de GÃ¼emes, 2nd Count of Revillagigedo, the Viceroy of New Spain.

Although the islands themselves have no state highways, the ferry routes serving the islands are designated as part of the state highway system.
