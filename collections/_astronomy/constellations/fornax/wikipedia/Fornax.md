**Fornax** (/ˈfɔːrnæks/) is a constellation in the southern celestial hemisphere, partly ringed by the celestial river [Eridanus]({% include cc-url for="Eridanus (constellation) - Wikipedia" %}). Its name is Latin for furnace. It was named by French astronomer Nicolas Louis de Lacaille in 1756. Fornax is one of the 88 modern constellations.
