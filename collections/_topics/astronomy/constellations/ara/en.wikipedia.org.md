**Ara** (Latin for "the Altar") is a southern constellation between [Scorpius](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scorpius), [Telescopium](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telescopium), [Triangulum Australe](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triangulum_Australe), and [Norma](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norma_(constellation)). It was (as Βωμός, Bōmǒs) one of the Greek bulk (namely 48) described by the 2nd-century astronomer [Ptolemy](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptolemy), and it remains one of the 88 modern constellations designated by the International Astronomical Union.
