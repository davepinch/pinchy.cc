      Within a given supercluster, most galaxy motions will be directed inward, toward the center of mass. In the case of Laniakea, this gravitational focal point is called the [Great Attractor](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Attractor), and influences the motions of the [Local Group](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_Group) of galaxies, where the [Milky Way](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milky_Way) galaxy resides, and all others throughout the supercluster.
