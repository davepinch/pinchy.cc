**New Orleans** (/ˈɔːrl(i)ənz/ *OR-l(ee)ənz*, /ɔːrˈliːnz/ *or-LEENZ*, locally /ˈɔːrlənz/ *OR-lənz*; French: La Nouvelle-Orléans [la nuvɛlɔʁleɑ̃] is a consolidated city-parish located along the Mississippi River in the southeastern region of the U.S. state of Louisiana. With a population of 383,997 according to the 2020 U.S. census, it is the most populous city in Louisiana and the twelfth-most populous city in the Southeastern United States. Serving as a major port, New Orleans is considered an economic and commercial hub for the broader Gulf Coast region of the United States.
