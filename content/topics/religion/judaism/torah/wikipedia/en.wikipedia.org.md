  The **Torah** (/ˈtɔːrə, ˈtoʊrə/; Biblical Hebrew: תּוֹרָה, romanized: *tōrā*, lit. "instruction", "teaching", or "law") is the compilation of the first five books of the Hebrew Bible, namely the books of Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers and Deuteronomy. The Torah is also known as the **Pentateuch** (/ˈpɛntəˌt(j)uːk/) or the **Five Books of Moses**. In Rabbinical Jewish tradition it is also known as the **Written Torah** (תּוֹרָה שֶׁבִּכְתָב, Tōrā šebbīḵṯāv). If meant for liturgic purposes, it takes the form of a Torah scroll (Hebrew: ספר תורה Sefer Torah). If in bound book form, it is called *Chumash*, and is usually printed with the rabbinic commentaries (*perushim*).
