  The **King James Version** (**KJV**), also the **King James Bible** (**KJB**) and the **Authorized Version** (**AV**), is an Early Modern English translation of the Christian Bible for the Church of England, which was commissioned in 1604 and published in 1611, by sponsorship of King James VI and I. The 80 books of the King James Version include 39 books of the [Old Testament](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Testament/), 14 books of Apocrypha, and the 27 books of the [New Testament](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Testament/).
