  **Paul** (also named **Saul of Tarsus**; c. 5 – c. 64/65 AD), commonly known as **Paul the Apostle** and **Saint Paul**, was a Christian apostle who spread the teachings of [Jesus](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesus/) in the first-century world. Generally regarded as one of the most important figures of the Apostolic Age, he founded several Christian communities in Asia Minor and Europe from the mid-40s to the mid-50s AD.
