  The **Proterozoic** (IPA: /ˌproʊtərəˈzoʊɪk, ˌprɒt-, -əroʊ-, -trə-, -troʊ-/ *PROH-tər-ə-ZOH-ik*, *PROT-*, *-⁠ər-oh-*, *-⁠trə-*, *-⁠troh-*) is the third of the four geologic eons of [Earth](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth/)'s history, spanning the time interval from 2500 to 538.8 Mya, the longest eon of the Earth's geologic time scale. It is preceded by the [Archean](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archean/) and followed by the [Phanerozoic](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phanerozoic/), and is the most recent part of the Precambrian "supereon".
