  The **Neoarchean** (/ˌniːoʊ.ɑːrˈkiːən/ *NEE-oh-ar-KEE-ən*; also spelled **Neoarchaean**) is the last geologic era in the [Archean](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archean/) Eon that spans from 2800 to 2500 million years ago—the period being defined chronometrically and not referencing a specific level in a rock section on [Earth](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth/). The era is marked by major developments in complex life and continental formation.
