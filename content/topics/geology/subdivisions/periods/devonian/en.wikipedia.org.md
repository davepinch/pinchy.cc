  The **Devonian** (/dɪˈvoʊni.ən, dɛ-/ də-VOH-nee-ən, deh-) is a geologic period and system of the [Paleozoic](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleozoic/) era during the [Phanerozoic](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phanerozoic/) eon, spanning 60.3 million years from the end of the preceding [Silurian](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silurian/) period at 419.2 million years ago (Ma), to the beginning of the succeeding [Carboniferous](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carboniferous/) period at 358.9 Ma. It is named after Devon, South West England, where rocks from this period were first studied.
