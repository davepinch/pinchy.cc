  The **Mesoarchean** (/ˌmiːzoʊ.ɑːrˈkiːən, ˌmɛzoʊ-/ *MEE-zoh-ar-KEE-ən*, *MEZ-oh-*, also spelled **Mesoarchaean**) is a geologic era in the [Archean](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archean/) Eon, spanning 3,200 to 2,800 million years ago, which contains the first evidence of modern-style plate subduction and expansion of microbial life. The era is defined chronometrically and is not referenced to a specific level in a rock section on [Earth](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth/).
