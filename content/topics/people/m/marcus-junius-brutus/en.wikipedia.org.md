  **Marcus Junius Brutus** (/ˈbruːtəs/; Latin pronunciation: [ˈmaːrkʊs juːniʊs ˈbruːtʊs]; c. 85 BC – 23 October 42 BC) was a Roman politician, orator, and the most famous of the assassins of Julius Caesar. After being adopted by a relative, he used the name **Quintus Servilius Caepio Brutus**, which was retained as his legal name. He is often referred to simply as **Brutus**.
