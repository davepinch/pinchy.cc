  The **Skagit River** (/ˈskædʒɪt/ SKAJ-it) is a river in southwestern [British Columbia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Columbia/) in [Canada](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canada/) and northwestern [Washington](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_(state)/) in the [United States](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States/), approximately 150 mi (240 km) long. The river and its tributaries drain an area of 1.7 million acres (690,000 hectares) of the [Cascade Range](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cascade_Range/) along the northern end of [Puget Sound](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puget_Sound/) and flows into the sound.
