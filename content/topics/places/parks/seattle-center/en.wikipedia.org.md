  **Seattle Center** is an arts, educational, tourism and entertainment center in [Seattle](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle/), [Washington](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_(state)/), [United States](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States/). Spanning an area of 74 acres (30 ha), it was originally built for the 1962 World's Fair. Its landmark feature is the 605 ft (184 m) tall [Space Needle](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Space_Needle/), which at the time of its completion was the tallest building west of the [Mississippi River](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_River/). Seattle Center is located just north of [Belltown](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belltown,_Seattle/) in the Uptown neighborhood.
