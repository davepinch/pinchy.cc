  **Woodland Park** is a 90.9-acre (36.8 ha) public park in [Seattle](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle/)'s [Phinney Ridge](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phinney_Ridge,_Seattle/) and [Green Lake](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Lake,_Seattle/) neighborhoods that originated as the estate of Guy C. Phinney, lumber mill owner and real estate developer. Phinney died in 1893, and in 1902, the Olmsted Brothers firm of [Boston](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boston/) was hired to design the city's parks, including Woodland Park.
