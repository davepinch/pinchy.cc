  The **Columbia Center**, formerly named the **Bank of America Tower** and **Columbia Seafirst Center**, is a skyscraper in downtown [Seattle](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle/), [Washington](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_(state)/), [United States](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States/). The 76-story structure is the tallest building in Seattle and the state of Washington, reaching a height of 933 ft (284 m). At the time of its completion, the Columbia Center was the tallest structure on the West Coast; as of 2017, it is the fourth-tallest, behind buildings in [Los Angeles](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Los_Angeles/) and [San Francisco](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Francisco/).
