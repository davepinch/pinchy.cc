  The **Suez Canal** (Arabic: قَنَاةُ ٱلسُّوَيْسِ, Qanāt as-Suwais) is an artificial sea-level waterway in [Egypt](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt/), connecting the [Mediterranean Sea](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea/) to the [Red Sea](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea/) through the Isthmus of Suez and dividing [Africa](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa/) and [Asia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia/) (and by extension, the Sinai Peninsula from the rest of Egypt). The 193.30-kilometre-long (120.11 mi) canal is a key trade route between [Europe](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe/) and Asia.
