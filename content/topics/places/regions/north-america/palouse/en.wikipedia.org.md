  The **Palouse** (/pəˈluːs/ *pə-LOOSS*) is a distinct geographic region of the northwestern United States, encompassing parts of north central [Idaho](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idaho/), southeastern [Washington](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_(state)/), and, by some definitions, parts of northeast [Oregon](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oregon/). It is a major agricultural area, primarily producing wheat and legumes. Situated about 160 miles (260 km) north of the Oregon Trail, the region experienced rapid growth in the late 19th century.
