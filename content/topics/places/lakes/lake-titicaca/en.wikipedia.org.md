  **Lake Titicaca** (/tɪtɪˈkɑːkə/; Spanish: *Lago Titicaca* [ˈlaɣo titiˈkaka]; Quechua: *Titiqaqa Qucha*) is a large freshwater lake in the Andes mountains on the border of [Bolivia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bolivia/) and [Peru](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peru/). It is often called the highest navigable lake in the world. Titicaca is the largest lake in [South America](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_America/) both in terms of the volume of water and surface area. It has a surface elevation of 3,812 m (12,507 ft).
