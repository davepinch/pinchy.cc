  **Lake Wenatchee** is a glacier- and snowmelt-fed lake situated in the Wenatchee National Forest on the eastern slopes of the [Cascades Mountain Range](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cascade_Range/) in the state of [Washington](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_(state)/). Lake Wenatchee covers 2,480 acres (1,000 ha) and reaches a depth of 244 feet (74 m). Lake Wenatchee is the source of the Wenatchee River. Its main tributaries are the White River and the Little Wenatchee River. At its eastern end, the lake is breasted by Lake Wenatchee State Park. Lake Wenatchee State Airport, an unimproved grass and dirt landing strip, is adjacent to the north side of the state park.
