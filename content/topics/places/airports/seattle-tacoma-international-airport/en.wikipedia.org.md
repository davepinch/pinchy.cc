  **Seattle–Tacoma International Airport** (IATA: **SEA**, ICAO: **KSEA**, FAA LID: **SEA**), branded as **SEA Airport** and also referred to as **Sea–Tac** (/ˈsiːtæk/), is the primary commercial airport serving the Seattle metropolitan area in the U.S. state of [Washington](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_(state)/). It is in the city of [SeaTac](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SeaTac,_Washington/), which was named after the airport's nickname “Sea-Tac”, approximately 14 miles (23 km) south of Downtown Seattle and 18 miles (29 km) north-northeast of Downtown Tacoma. The airport is the busiest in the [Pacific Northwest](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Northwest/) region of [North America](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_America/) and is owned by Port of Seattle.
