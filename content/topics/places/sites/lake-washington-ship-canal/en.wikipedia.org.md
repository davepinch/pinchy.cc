  The **Lake Washington Ship Canal**, which runs through the city of [Seattle](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle/), connects the fresh water body of [Lake Washington](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Washington/) with the salt water inland sea of [Puget Sound](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puget_Sound/). The Hiram M. Chittenden Locks accommodate the approximately 20-foot (6.1 m) difference in water level between Lake Washington and the sound.
