  The **Gulf of Aqaba** (Arabic: خَلِيج الْعَقَبَة, romanized: Khalīj al-ʿAqaba) or Gulf of Eilat (Hebrew: מפרץ אילת, romanized: Mifrátz Eilát) is a large gulf at the northern tip of the [Red Sea](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea/), east of the Sinai Peninsula and west of the Arabian Peninsula. Its coastline is divided among four countries: [Egypt](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt/), [Israel](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel/), [Jordan](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan/), and [Saudi Arabia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia/). The northernmost coral reef in the world is situated near the Eilat shore.
