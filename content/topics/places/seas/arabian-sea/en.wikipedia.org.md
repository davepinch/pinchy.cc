  The **Arabian Sea** (Arabic: بَحرُ ٱلْعَرَبْ, romanized: baḥr al-ʿarab) is a region of sea in the northern [Indian Ocean](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean/), bounded on the west by the Arabian Peninsula, [Gulf of Aden](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Aden/) and Guardafui Channel, on the northwest by [Gulf of Oman](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Oman/) and [Iran](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran/), on the north by [Pakistan](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan/), on the east by [India](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India/), and on the southeast by the Laccadive Sea and the Maldives, on the southwest by [Somalia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Somalia/). Its total area is 3,862,000 km2 (1,491,000 sq mi) and its maximum depth is 4,652 meters (15,262 feet). The Gulf of Aden in the west connects the [Arabian Sea](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Aden/) to the [Red Sea](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea/) through the strait of Bab-el-Mandeb, and the [Gulf of Oman](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Oman/) is in the northwest, connecting it to the [Persian Gulf](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_Gulf/).
