  The **Adriatic Sea** (/ˌeɪdriˈætɪk/) is a body of water separating the Italian Peninsula from the Balkan Peninsula. The Adriatic is the northernmost arm of the [Mediterranean Sea](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea/), extending from the Strait of Otranto (where it connects to the [Ionian Sea](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionian_Sea/)) to the northwest and the Po Valley. The countries with coasts on the Adriatic are [Albania](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albania/), [Bosnia and Herzegovina](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina/), [Croatia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Croatia/), [Italy](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy/), [Montenegro](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montenegro/), and [Slovenia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slovenia/).
