  **Tennessee** (/ˌtɛnɪˈsiː/ *TEN-ih-SEE*, locally /ˈtɛnɪsi/ *TEN-iss-ee*), officially the **State of Tennessee**, is a landlocked state in the [Southeastern](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeastern_United_States) region of the [United States](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States). Tennessee is the 36th-largest by area and the 15th-most populous of the 50 states. It is bordered by [Kentucky](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kentucky) to the north, [Virginia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virginia) to the northeast, [North Carolina](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Carolina) to the east, [Georgia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)), [Alabama](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alabama), and [Mississippi](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi) to the south, [Arkansas](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arkansas) to the southwest, and [Missouri](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Missouri) to the northwest. Tennessee is geographically, culturally, and legally divided into three Grand Divisions of East, Middle, and West Tennessee. Nashville is the state's capital and largest city, and anchors its largest metropolitan area. Other major cities include Memphis, Knoxville, Chattanooga, and Clarksville. Tennessee's population as of the 2020 United States census is approximately 6.9 million.
