  **Mississippi** (/ˌmɪsɪˈsɪpi/) is a state in the [Southeastern](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeastern_United_States) region of the [United States](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States), bordered to the north by [Tennessee](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tennessee); to the east by [Alabama](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alabama); to the south by the [Gulf of Mexico](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico); to the southwest by [Louisiana](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louisiana); and to the northwest by [Arkansas](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arkansas). Mississippi's western boundary is largely defined by the [Mississippi River](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_River). Mississippi is the 32nd largest by area and 35th-most populous of the 50 U.S. states and has the lowest per-capita income in the United States. Jackson is both the state's capital and largest city. Greater Jackson is the state's most populous metropolitan area, with a population of 591,978 in 2020.
