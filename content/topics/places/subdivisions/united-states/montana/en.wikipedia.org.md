  **Montana** (/mɒnˈtænə/) is a state in the Mountain division of the [Western United States](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_United_States/). It is bordered by [Idaho](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idaho/) to the west; [North Dakota](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Dakota/) and [South Dakota](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Dakota/) to the east; [Wyoming](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wyoming/) to the south; and by the Canadian provinces of Alberta, [British Columbia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Columbia/), and Saskatchewan to the north. It is the fourth-largest state by area, the eighth-least populous state, and the third-least densely populated state. Its state capital is Helena. The western half of Montana contains numerous mountain ranges, while the eastern half is characterized by western prairie terrain and badlands, with smaller mountain ranges found throughout the state. In all, 77 named ranges are part of the Rocky Mountains.
