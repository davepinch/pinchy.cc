  **Mason County** is a county located in the U.S. state of [Washington](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_(state)/). As of the 2020 census, the population was 65,726. The county seat and only incorporated city is Shelton. The county was formed out of [Thurston County](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thurston_County,_Washington/) on March 13, 1854. Originally named Sawamish County, it took its present name in 1864 in honor of Charles H. Mason, the first Secretary of Washington Territory.
