  **Georgia** (Georgian: საქართველო, romanized: *sakartvelo*, IPA: [sakʰartʰʷelo]) is a transcontinental country in [Eastern Europe](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Europe/) and [West Asia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Asia/). It is part of the Caucasus region, bounded by the [Black Sea](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea/) to the west, [Russia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russia/) to the north and northeast, [Turkey](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey/) to the southwest, [Armenia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenia/) to the south, and [Azerbaijan](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan/) to the southeast. Georgia covers an area of 69,700 square kilometres (26,900 sq mi). It has a population of 3.7 million, of which over a third live in the capital and largest city, Tbilisi. Georgians, who are indigenous to the region, constitute a majority and a titular nation in Georgia.
