  **Moldova** (/mɒlˈdoʊvə/ *mol-DOH-və*, sometimes UK: /ˈmɒldəvə/ MOL-də-və; Romanian pronunciation: [molˈdova]), officially the **Republic of Moldova** (Romanian: *Republica Moldova*), is a landlocked country in [Eastern Europe](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Europe/), on the northeastern corner of the Balkans. The country spans a total of 33,483 km2 (13,067 sq mi) and has a population of approximately 2.5 million as of January 2023. Moldova is bordered by [Romania](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romania/) to the west and [Ukraine](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ukraine/) to the north, east, and south. The unrecognised breakaway state of Transnistria lies across the Dniester river on the country's eastern border with Ukraine. Moldova is a unitary parliamentary representative democratic republic with its capital in Chișinău, the country's largest city and main cultural and commercial centre.
