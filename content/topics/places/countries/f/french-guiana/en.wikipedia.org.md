  **French Guiana** (/ɡiˈɑːnə/ or /ɡiˈænə/; French: Guyane, [ɡɥijan]; French Guianese Creole: Lagwiyann or Gwiyann, [la.ɡwi.jãn]) is an overseas department and region of [France](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France/) located on the northern coast of [South America](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_America/) in the Guianas and the West Indies. Bordered by [Suriname](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suriname/) to the west and [Brazil](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazil/) to the east and south, French Guiana covers a total area of 84,000 km2 (32,000 sq mi) and a land area of 83,534 km^2 (32,253 sq mi), and is inhabited by 295,385 people.
