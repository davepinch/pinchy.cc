  **Uruguay** (/ˈjʊərəɡwaɪ/ YOOR-ə-gwy, Spanish: [uɾuˈɣwaj]), officially the **Oriental Republic of Uruguay** (Spanish: República Oriental del Uruguay), is a country in [South America](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_America/). It shares borders with [Argentina](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argentina/) to its west and southwest and [Brazil](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazil/) to its north and northeast, while bordering the Río de la Plata to the south and the [Atlantic Ocean](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean/) to the southeast. It is part of the Southern Cone region of South America. Uruguay covers an area of approximately 176,215 square kilometres (68,037 sq mi) and has a population of around 3.4 million, of whom nearly 2 million live in the metropolitan area of its capital and largest city, Montevideo.
