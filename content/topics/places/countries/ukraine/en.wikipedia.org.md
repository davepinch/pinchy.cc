  **Ukraine** (/juːˈkreɪn/ yoo-KRAYN; Ukrainian: Україна, romanized: *Ukraina*, pronounced [ʊkrɐˈjinɐ]) is a country in Eastern Europe. It is the second-largest European country after [Russia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russia/), which borders it to the east and northeast. It also borders [Belarus](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belarus/) to the north; [Poland](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poland/), [Slovakia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slovakia/), and [Hungary](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungary/) to the west; and [Romania](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romania/) and [Moldova](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moldova/) to the southwest; with a coastline along the [Black Sea](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea/) and the [Sea of Azov](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Azov/) to the south and southeast. Kyiv is the nation's capital and largest city, followed by Kharkiv, Dnipro and Odesa. Ukraine's official language is Ukrainian; Russian is also widely spoken, especially in the east and south.
