  **Ethiopia**, officially the **Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia**, is a landlocked country located in the Horn of Africa region of East Africa. It shares borders with [Eritrea](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eritrea/) to the north, [Djibouti](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Djibouti/) to the northeast, [Somalia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Somalia/) to the East, [Kenya](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenya/) to the South, [South Sudan](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Sudan/) to the West, and [Sudan](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan/) to the Northwest. Ethiopia covers a land area of 1,112,000 square kilometres (472,000 sq. miles). As of 2024, it is home to around 129 million inhabitants, making it the 13th-most populous country in the world, the 2nd-most populous in Africa after Nigeria, and the most populated landlocked country on Earth. The national capital and largest city, Addis Ababa, lies several kilometres west of the East African Rift that splits the country into the African and Somali tectonic plates.
