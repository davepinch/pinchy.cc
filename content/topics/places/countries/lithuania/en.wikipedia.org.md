  **Lithuania** (/ˌlɪθjuˈeɪniə/ *LITH-yoo-AYN-ee-ə*; Lithuanian: *Lietuva* [lʲɪɛtʊˈvɐ]), officially the **Republic of Lithuania** (Lithuanian: *Lietuvos Respublika* [lʲɪɛtʊˈvoːs rʲɛsˈpʊblʲɪkɐ]), is a country in the Baltic region of [Europe](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe/). It is one of three Baltic states and lies on the eastern shore of the Baltic Sea. It borders [Latvia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latvia/) to the north, [Belarus](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belarus/) to the east and south, [Poland](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poland/) to the south, and [Russia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russia/) to the southwest, with a maritime border with [Sweden](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sweden/) to the west. Lithuania covers an area of 65,300 km2 (25,200 sq mi), with a population of 2.86 million. Its capital and largest city is Vilnius; other major cities are Kaunas, Klaipėda, Šiauliai and Panevėžys. Lithuanians belong to the ethnolinguistic group of the Balts and speak Lithuanian, one of only a few living Baltic languages, and the most widely spoken.
