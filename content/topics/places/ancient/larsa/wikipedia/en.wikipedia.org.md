  **Larsa** (Sumerian: 𒌓𒀕𒆠, romanized: *UD.UNUGKI*, read *Larsamki*), also referred to as **Larancha**/**Laranchon** (Gk. Λαραγχων) by Berossos and connected with the biblical Ellasar, was an important city-state of ancient Sumer, the center of the cult of the sun god Utu with his temple E-babbar. It lies some 25 km (16 mi) southeast of Uruk in Iraq's Dhi Qar Governorate, near the east bank of the Shatt-en-Nil canal at the site of the modern settlement **Tell as-Senkereh** or **Sankarah**.
