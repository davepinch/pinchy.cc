  **Northgate** is a neighborhood in north [Seattle](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle/), [Washington](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_(state)/), named for and surrounding Northgate Mall, the first covered mall in the [United States](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States/). Its north-south principal arterials are Roosevelt Way NE and Aurora Avenue N (SR 99), and its east-west principal arterials are NE Northgate Way and 130th Street. Minor arterials are College Way-Meridian Avenue N, 1st, 5th, and 15th avenues NE. [Interstate 5](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstate_5_in_Washington/) runs through the district. Besides the eponymous mall, the most characteristic distinctions of the area are [North Seattle College](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Seattle_College/) (NSC) and the south fork of the Thornton Creek watershed and Seattle Kraken Iceplex center.
