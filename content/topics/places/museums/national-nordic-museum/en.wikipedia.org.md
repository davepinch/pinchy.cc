  The **National Nordic Museum** (previously **Nordic Heritage Museum** and then **Nordic Museum**) is a museum in the [Ballard](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ballard,_Seattle/) neighborhood of [Seattle](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle/), [Washington](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_(state)/), [United States](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States/), dedicated to the Nordic history, art, culture, and the heritage of the area's Nordic immigrants. It was founded in 1980 as the Nordic Heritage Museum, moved into a permanent, purpose-built facility in 2018 named the Nordic Museum, and was designated as the National Nordic Museum in 2019. The museum serves as a community gathering place and shares Nordic culture by exhibiting art and objects, preserving collections, and providing educational and cultural experiences from Danish, Finnish, Icelandic, Norwegian and Swedish Americans. The geographical region covered by the Museum includes the entire Nordic region ([Denmark](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denmark/), [Finland](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finland/), [Iceland](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iceland/), [Norway](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norway/), [Sweden](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sweden/), [Estonia](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Estonia/), the regions of the [Faroe Islands](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faroe_Islands/), [Greenland](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenland/), and [Åland](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Åland/), and the cultural region of [Sápmi](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sápmi/)).
