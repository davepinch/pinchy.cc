  **Silicon dioxide**, also known as **silica**, is an oxide of [silicon](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon/) with the chemical formula SiO2, commonly found in nature as [quartz](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quartz/). In many parts of the world, silica is the major constituent of sand. Silica is abundant as it comprises several minerals and synthetic products. All forms are white or colorless, although impure samples can be colored.
