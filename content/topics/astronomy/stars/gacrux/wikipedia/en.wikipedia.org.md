excerpt: >-
  **Gacrux** is the third-brightest star in the southern constellation of [Crux](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crux/), the Southern Cross. It has the Bayer designation **Gamma Crucis**, which is Latinised from **γ Crucis** and abbreviated **Gamma Cru** or **γ Cru**. With an apparent visual magnitude of +1.63, it is the 26th brightest star in the night sky. A line from the two "Pointers", [Alpha Centauri](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alpha_Centauri/) through Beta Centauri, leads to within 1° north of this star. Using parallax measurements made during the Hipparcos mission, it is located at a distance of 88.6 light-years (27.2 parsecs) from the [Sun](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun/). It is the nearest M-type red giant star to the Sun.
