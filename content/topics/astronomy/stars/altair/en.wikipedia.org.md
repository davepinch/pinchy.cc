  **Altair** is the brightest star in the constellation of [Aquila](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aquila_(constellation)/) and the twelfth-brightest star in the night sky. It has the Bayer designation Alpha Aquilae, which is Latinised from **α Aquilae** and abbreviated **Alpha Aql** or **α Aql**. Altair is an [A-type](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A-type_main-sequence_star/) main-sequence star with an apparent visual magnitude of 0.77 and is one of the vertices of the [Summer Triangle](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Summer_Triangle/) asterism; the other two vertices are marked by [Deneb](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deneb/) and [Vega](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vega). It is located at a distance of 16.7 light-years (5.1 parsecs) from the [Sun](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun/).  Altair is currently in the [G-cloud](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G-Cloud/)—a nearby interstellar cloud, an accumulation of gas and dust.
