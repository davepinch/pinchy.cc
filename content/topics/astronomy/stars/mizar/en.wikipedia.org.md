  **Mizar** /ˈmaɪzɑːr/ is a second-magnitude star in the handle of the Big Dipper asterism in the constellation of [Ursa Major](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ursa_Major/). It has the Bayer designation **ζ Ursae Majoris** (Latinised as **Zeta Ursae Majoris**). It forms a well-known naked eye double star with the fainter star [Alcor](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alcor_(star)/), and is itself a quadruple star system. The Mizar and Alcor system lies about 83 light-years away from the [Sun](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun/), as measured by the Hipparcos astrometry satellite, and is part of the [Ursa Major Moving Group](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ursa_Major_moving_group/).
