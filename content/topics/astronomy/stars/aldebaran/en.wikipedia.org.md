  **Aldebaran** (Arabic: الدَّبَران, lit. 'The Follower') is a star located in the [zodiac](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zodiac/) constellation of [Taurus](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taurus_(constellation)/). It has the Bayer designation α Tauri, which is Latinized to Alpha Tauri and abbreviated Alpha Tau or α Tau. Aldebaran varies in brightness from an apparent visual magnitude 0.75 down to 0.95, making it the brightest star in the constellation, as well as (typically) the fourteenth-brightest star in the night sky. It is positioned at a distance of approximately 65 light-years from the [Sun](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun/). The star lies along the line of sight to the nearby Hyades cluster.
