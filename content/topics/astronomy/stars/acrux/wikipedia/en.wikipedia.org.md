  **Acrux** is the brightest star in the southern constellation of [Crux](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crux/). It has the Bayer designation **α Crucis**, which is Latinised to **Alpha Crucis** and abbreviated **Alpha Cru** or **α Cru**. With a combined visual magnitude of +0.76, it is the 13th-brightest star in the night sky. It is the most southerly star of the asterism known as the Southern Cross and is the southernmost first-magnitude star, 2.3 degrees more southerly than [Alpha Centauri](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alpha_Centauri/). This system is located at a distance of 321 light-years from the [Sun](/en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun/).
