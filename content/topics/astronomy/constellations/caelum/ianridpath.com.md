He introduced it on his map of the southern stars published in 1756, where he gave it the French name *les Burins* (although in the accompanying star catalogue it was listed as *Burin*, singular). On the second edition of the map in 1763 this was Latinized to Caelum Scalptorium. In 1844 the English astronomer John Herschel proposed shortening the name to Caelum. Francis Baily adopted this suggestion in his *British Association Catalogue* of 1845, and it has been known as Caelum ever since.
