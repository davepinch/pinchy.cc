title: "Discourse on the Method, part 1, ¶ 2-1"
author: René Descartes
snippet: "For myself, I have never fancied my mind to be in any respect more perfect than those of the generality; on the contrary, I have often wished that I were equal to some others in promptitude of thought, or in clearness and distinctness of imagination, or in fulness and readiness of memory."
