title: "Upper Dry Gulch Natural Area Preserve (dnr.wa.gov)"
excerpt: >-
  This 3,087-acre preserve protects the largest known population of Whited's milkvetch, a state-listed "endangered" plant species that is found only within a 3-square-mile area. Situated in the arid mountains southeast of Wenatchee, this site is dominated by a native shrub and bunchgrass (shrub-steppe) ecosystem that is recovering from past land uses. The drainage that flows through the site carries water seasonally and is subject to torrential flows during spring. The loggerhead shrike, a bird species known to be highly dependent on shrub-steppe habitat, is also known to frequent this area.
